Vikram Sarabhai

Father of the Indian space program

Here's a timeline of Vikram Sarabhai's life:

  • 1887 - Born in Erode, Mysore, India
  • 1889 - Moved to Kanchipuram, Chennai due to smallpox outbreak.
  • 1892 - Enrolled in a local school, his family enlisted a local constable to make sure he attended school.
  • 1897 - Passed his primary examinations in English, Tamil, geography, and arithmetic with the best scores in the district. Entered Town Higher Secondary School, where he encountered formal mathematics for the first time.
  • 1904 - Independently developed and investigated the Bernoulli numbers and calculated the Euler–Mascheroni constant up to 15 decimal places. Graduated from Town Higher Secondary School and was awarded the K. Ranganatha Rao prize for mathematics.
  • 1906 - Failed his Fellow of Arts exam and without an FA degree, he left college and continued to pursue independent research in mathematics, living in extreme poverty.
  • 1910 - Gained recognition in Madras's mathematical circles and became a researcher at the University of Madras.
  • 1911 - Published a 17-page paper titled "Some Properties of Bernoulli's Numbers" containing three proofs, two corollaries, and three conjectures.
  • 1913 - Received a letter from Hardy expressing interest in his work and was invited to England. Ramanujan refused to leave his country.
  • 1914 - Traveled to England and began collaborating with Hardy and Littlewood.
  • 1916 - Awarded a Bachelor of Arts by Research degree for his work on highly composite numbers.
  • 1917 - Elected to the London Mathematical Society.
  • 1918 - Elected a Fellow of the Royal Society and a Fellow of Trinity College, Cambridge.
  • 1919 - Diagnosed with tuberculosis and a severe vitamin deficiency.
  • 1920 - Passed away at the age of 32 in Kumbakonam, Madras Presidency.
